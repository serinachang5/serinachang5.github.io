Serina Chang is a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Stanford University. Her research focuses on large-scale human networks, such as mobility networks, supply chains, and query-click graphs from search engines. She develops methods in network science and machine learning to leverage these networks and tackle complex policy problems in public health and society. Her work has been published in venues including Nature, PNAS, KDD, AAAI, and EMNLP and featured in media outlets such as The New York Times and The Washington Post. Her work is also recognized by the KDD 2021 Best Paper Award, Meta PhD Fellowship, NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, and CRA Outstanding Undergraduate Researcher Award. Previously, Serina received her B.A. at Columbia University, where she studied Computer Science and Sociology.
